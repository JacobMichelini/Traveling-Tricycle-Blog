Bryce Arrives!

It is at this point in our journey that we venture back into the crazy of Bangkok and meet up with our buddy Bryce, fresh off the plane from the states. Bryce had previously spent several months all around SE Asia, so we were very happy to have him along as someone who hopefully had more of an idea of what the fuck he was doing than we did. After some confusion with a legally blind or perhaps illiterate taxi driver we met up at a lovely guest house Bryce was familiar with. You'll just have to trust us that it was nice and awesomely decorated because I was too lazy to take any pictures of it.

Bryce immediately demonstrated his travel skills and suggested we take a quick trip up to the city of Ayutthaya. Ayutthaya used to be a huge big deal in ancient times (one of the largest cities in the world in 1700's), but it has now shrunk to a smallish town surrounded by canals and quite a lot of cool looking ruins. Another perk of riding the train from Bangkok to Ayutthaya is that a ticket is almost comically inexpensive, around sixty cents! This sounded pretty good to us, so we hopped on and meandered around the area for a few days, touring the markets and the abandoned temple complexes. The heads of the Buddha statues were knocked off by invading Burmese ages ago, in case you were wondering. Wasn't us I promise.

Holy Shit

Did I mention it was absolutely blisteringly hot with basically no shade? Now I did. Hillary especially was approaching melting, as we had to wear long, culturally respectful clothing when visiting the temples, and hers was less than breathable. Even the feral dogs were smart enought to stay out of the sun. Taking shelter in the darker of the temple nooks and crannies proved to be a bad idea unless you enjoy being crapped on by hundreds of roosting bats. We had to cut our adventure a bit short and retreat to the showers to avoid heatstroke.

Crepuscular Sightseeing

We relaxed for a while back at our room and took several showers, before deciding to head out again at dusk. We biked a few miles out of town to a different temple we had heard was good to view the sunset from. The ride was a bit more hectic and trafficky than we had bargained for, but once we made it out of the busy city streets the rural areas were quite green and scenic. This temple was located through a small raised village in the midst of a what was basically a swamp of rice fields and ponds. It was very pretty on the way in while the sun was shining, but on the way back in the dark we had to keep our mouths shut to avoid eating thousands of mosquitos. Unfortunately they had no such qualms about eating us. The temple itself is pretty spectacular and large, it reminded me a lot of an Aztec or Mayan step pyramid. The sunset was also very pretty, and we were the only visitors. The clouds in SE Asia are absolutely stunning for some reason, far superior to anything I've ever seen in the US. The pictures do not do them justice in this case.
