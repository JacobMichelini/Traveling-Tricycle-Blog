Chiang Mai Sunday Nightmarket

Chiang Mai has several different open air markets running daily or nightly in various parts of the city, but every sunday they throw a extra large extravaganza all the way down the main street of the old city. It's crowded as hell and sells just about every useless but pretty knick-knack you can imagine, as well as copious quantities of interesting and probably fried food. Take a look.

Slow Going

The market is absolutely packed in some parts, when the crowd moves through a tight spot it can feel like moving 50 feet takes forever. The brightly colored candy up there is a traditional Thai marzipan, and it's much prettier than it is good to eat. We agreed it was basically tasteless mealy paste, but hey its like six cents a piece.

The big block of black goo directly above is called grass jelly. It's used mostly to make drinks, mostly like bubble tea type stuff with chunks. It's alright, tastes kind of like bitterish tea flavored jello. Nothing to write home about, although I guess I sort of am.

Down below are some cute little tuk-tuks made out of drink cans. Tuk-tuks (funny little three wheeled taxis) are an emblematic part of Thailand, but we can't really see a reason to use one ever. They are just as expensive as a real metered taxi, slower, with no airconditioning, and since they have no meter every driver is doing their best to scam you or overcharge their fare. Maybe its a better deal if you are a local, but honestly I didn't see many locals using them either. If you're a real man and want to experience local transport you get a motorbike taxi and either get there in half the time or die in transit (about 50/50).

Asian Food

I think I kind of had an impression of Asian food being healthier than American, but at least in the street food department this is not the case. The vast majority is either fried or consists in large part of chunks of fat or has more sugar than a two-liter of coke, or all of these things combined. The sugar in particular is jarring to a western palate. Desert food or juice is sickeningly sweet. Exclusively savory food is uncommon. For instance Hillary bought a powdered donut and found it was stuffed with chicken. Interesting.
